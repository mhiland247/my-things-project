About Me

I am a Colorado native and growing up in a mountain town I learned about balancing an economy based on tourism with a high quality of life for residents. Estes Park has been my home for the past 5 years and I love adventuring daily in the RMNP. Some of my favorite activities are running up Longs Peak, ice skating Sky Pond, finding powder in Hidden Valley or taking an excursion on my cross country skis or mountain bike.

I graduated from the University of Colorado in Boulder with two degrees, one in Business and one in Integrated Physiology (Kinesiology). I currently work for an endurance training software company where I create the strategy and implementation of our marketing and communication to athletes and coaches all over the world.

To learn more about my professional experience visit my LinkedIn.

Issues

My position on issues facing Estes Park.

COMMUNITY

Quality of Life for Residents

As our tourism grows so should our quality of life for our residents. We need to make sure we have the necessary amenities that people look for when they decide where to live, work, retire and raise a family. We need places to gather as a community, we need community events planned for locals, we need places to park in the summer, we need solutions to the congestion during the peak season, and we need a variety of businesses and offerings to meet our needs.

OPPORTUNITY

Economic Development

We live in one of the most unique mountain towns in Colorado, with our proximity to the front range and our natural beauty, we should be able to easily attract and retain new businesses. We can leverage our advantages and our partnerships with other organizations in town to overcome the barriers to starting a business and to enhance our current businesses. We need data from business owners, visitors and residents to create a strategy to move forward.

PLANNING FOR THE FUTURE

We need to start planning not for the next tourist season but the next 20 years. We should be actively pursuing alternative forms of transportation and renewable energy. We need a bike lane downtown, more pedestrian friendly areas, and more winter activities, to name just a few. We have many stategic plans that offer visions of what could be, it is time to start making those a reality.

Paid Parking

Managing the Visitor Experience

As our tourism continues to grow and the inconvenience to residents and visitors increases, we must manage parking to make the experience better for both. We need to think through the advantages of implementing paid parking downtown. It offers an opportunity to capitalize on our visitors to help maintain our town and invest back into our community.

Vacation Rentals

I support vacation rentals because I know they are critical to our economy and the visitor experience. However, I believe there must also be an acceptable balance to the community. The management and regulation of vacation rentals in residential areas must be strictly enforced. We must do everything we can to minimize any impact to locals who live nearby vacation rentals.

Housing

Workforce, Affordable

There are developments in the near future to address affordable housing, so I want to ensure these are done well. I will push to include the public throughout the process by communicating well in advance. I want the town to create partnerships with businesses and the housing authority to create an effective system that ensures we are providing housing for people who work in our town.

FloodPlain Map

I will do whatever I can to make sure we have the best floodplain map that is vetted and acceptable. The impacts to downtown could be substantial and I want to ensure the town board is doing what it can to ease the impact.

RMNP

Visitation, Fees

The proposed fee increase to our park is not the optimal solution. However, I would like to see our community discuss the impacts of the park being full much of the year and the reality of a significant change to access the park in the near future. We must help drive forward a better visitor experience for both locals and guests.

BroadBand

The number one priority should be redundancy, we need another fiber line connecting us outside our town to prevent another total outage. The second priority is both capacity and accessibility, we need to make sure we have enough bandwidth to support our current needs and any future growth, while also expanding access.

A Home for ChildCare

In order for Estes Park to be a sustainable place for families we need to partner and work closely with the Family Advisory Board, EVICS, and the EDC childcare task force. We need to address the need for infant childcare immediately. The children are the future of Estes Park and we must do whatever we can to find a home for them.

Stormwater Masterplan

We need to prepare for the future and adopt the stormwater masterplan. This plan will provide sustainable drainage and flood mitigation solutions for both new and existing businesses and developments. However, before any action on funding is taken the public needs to understand what it is and how it will impact them. I will support exploring funding options as a utility is only one way. I will also push to prioritize the highest impact upgrades and see if the town can fund these first.

Capital Improvements

Thanks to the leadership of our Mayor, we have a capital improvement plan. This empowers the town board to prioritize the inevitable future upgrades to our infrastructure and provides the ability to set aside funds to address these as needed. Using this as a tool to continue to improve and maintain our town will be key.
